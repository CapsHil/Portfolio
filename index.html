About Me.

Always facinated by the new technologies, it is naturally that I went to this field of activity. Rich in what I have learned in ESIEA and the Pierre et Marie Curie University, I am now moving towards Software Architecture and IoT Systems Architecture. This represents for me the perfect domain, mixing software development, network, hardware but also human relation. My different experiences within student associations and project management also allowed me to acquire solid managerial skills that I would like to use in my professional life.

AiC is an open-source project to industrialize the development of Android application thanks to a test solution. The goal is to create an Android virtual machine which will then be deployed on a cloud computing infrastructure. I was in charge of the test framework architecture and development

AiC 2.0

AiC 2.0

Android in Cloud project

Form@ter is a project of the IPGP in collaboration with the European Space Agency, aiming to use satellite photographs to determine surveys of topographic deformations. I was in charge of setting up and developing the architecture between the ESA data-center and the final interface presented to the researchers.

Form@ter

Topographic satellite mapping

Halfred is an open-source program which runs on Raspberry Pi. It communicates with all my self-build connected devices and checks my calendar to help me in my everyday life.

The ESIEA Colloque is a week of seminars in a winter sports resort, combining interactions between 4th year students and companies through sporting and educational activities (conferences, workshops ...). I was first a member of the team, then project manager in order to carry out this event.

Atictendo is an arcade terminal simulator. Developed in C ++, it contains 9 classic arcade mini-games, revisited in order to become multiplayer. I added a DSL to facilitate the future creation of complementary mini-games.
